Project Name: Project Velocity
Industry: Retail
The Client Challenge: The client's flagship e-commerce storefront was built on a monolithic legacy CMS that took over 8 seconds to load on mobile devices. Traffic spikes during holiday sales routinely caused database locks and site crashes. Their marketing team was unable to deploy new campaigns quickly due to the tightly coupled frontend and backend, resulting in lost revenue and a high bounce rate.
Our Technical Solution: We architected a complete decoupling of their monolithic infrastructure, transitioning them to a headless commerce model. The new frontend was rebuilt entirely using Next.js to leverage server-side rendering (SSR) and static site generation (SSG) for maximum performance and SEO benefits. This frontend was hosted on Vercel, utilizing their edge network to serve cached content globally with minimal latency.
For the backend, we engineered a suite of microservices using Node.js and Express. These services handled discrete domains such as cart management, user authentication, and inventory. We utilized GraphQL as the API gateway layer, allowing the frontend to request exactly the data it needed and nothing more, which drastically reduced payload sizes over the network.
To handle high-volume transactional data, we migrated their primary product catalog to MongoDB, while keeping strictly relational data (like order ledgers) in PostgreSQL. Redis was introduced as a caching layer for highly requested product endpoints, ensuring that backend database reads were minimized during high-traffic flash sales.
The ROI & Impact: Reduced mobile load times from 8.2 seconds to 1.4 seconds. Increased conversion rate by 18% during peak holiday traffic, and achieved zero downtime during Black Friday sales.
Total Project Cost: $320,000
