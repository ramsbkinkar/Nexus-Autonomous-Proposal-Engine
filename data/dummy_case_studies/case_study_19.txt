Project Name: Project Turbine
Industry: Energy & Renewables
The Client Challenge: An energy company operating offshore wind farms was losing millions annually to unexpected mechanical failures. Their existing system ingested turbine telemetry data (vibration, temperature, wind speed) via daily batch uploads. By the time data engineers processed the files to detect anomalies, gearboxes had often already failed, requiring expensive emergency maintenance and causing extended downtime.
Our Technical Solution: We engineered a massive real-time IoT data pipeline. We upgraded the edge computing modules on the wind turbines to stream high-frequency MQTT telemetry data continuously. This data was securely ingested via AWS IoT Core and immediately pushed into Amazon Kinesis Data Streams to buffer the massive throughput (over 200,000 events per second).
We deployed a Databricks environment utilizing Delta Live Tables to process the Kinesis streams in real-time. PySpark jobs were configured to clean the sensor data, handle missing values, and calculate rolling window aggregations. We then applied pre-trained machine learning models directly within the Databricks streaming pipeline to score the telemetry against known failure signatures.
When an anomaly crossed a critical threshold, the system triggered an AWS Lambda function that integrated with the company's enterprise asset management API, automatically generating a high-priority maintenance ticket. The raw and aggregated data was subsequently loaded into Snowflake to serve as a persistent, structured data warehouse for long-term climate modeling and hardware degradation analysis.
The ROI & Impact: Shifted maintenance strategy from reactive to predictive, preventing 14 major gearbox failures in the first year. Saved an estimated $2.8M in emergency repair costs and lost energy generation.
Total Project Cost: $650,000
