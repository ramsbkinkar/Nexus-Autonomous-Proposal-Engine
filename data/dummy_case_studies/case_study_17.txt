Project Name: Project Fulcrum
Industry: Retail & Omnichannel Commerce
The Client Challenge: A national retail chain with 400+ physical stores and a growing online presence was suffering from severe inventory discrepancies. Their e-commerce platform often sold items that were actually out of stock because the physical stores only ran batch inventory updates to the central server every 24 hours. This led to high cancellation rates, damaged brand reputation, and lost cross-selling opportunities.
Our Technical Solution: We led a complete digital transformation, starting with the implementation of an event-driven microservices architecture using Apache Kafka. We deployed IoT RFID scanners and smart-shelf sensors in the physical stores. Every time an item was purchased, moved, or restocked, an event was instantly streamed via Azure Event Hubs into our central Kafka clusters.
The e-commerce frontend was rebuilt from the ground up using Next.js, providing an ultra-fast, statically generated shopping experience. The backend was modularized into Node.js microservices hosted on Azure Kubernetes Service (AKS). The dedicated "Inventory Service" subscribed directly to the Kafka streams, maintaining a millisecond-accurate view of global stock levels in a Redis cache.
To handle historical analytics and supply chain forecasting, the Kafka event streams were also sunk into Snowflake via Snowpipe. This allowed the client's data engineering team to build complex predictive models using dbt (data build tool), ensuring regional distribution centers automatically restocked physical stores before they ran out of high-demand items.
The ROI & Impact: Decreased e-commerce order cancellation rates due to stockouts by 92%. Improved global inventory accuracy from 78% to 99.4%, resulting in a $3.2M recovery in otherwise lost sales.
Total Project Cost: $510,000
